⚡
WHT Payment Workflow
What happens every time you pay a supplier

1. Receive Invoice

Invoice from vendor with line items. Each line item must be classified by payment type to determine WHT rate.

2. Calculate WHT per Line Item

WHT = (Amount before VAT) × WHT rate. Different line items may have different rates. E.g., an invoice with both "rent" (5%) and "services" (3%) line items.

3. Pay Vendor (minus WHT)

Transfer = Invoice total − total WHT amount. You keep the WHT portion to remit to RD.

4. Issue WHT Certificate (50 Tawi)

Provide 2 copies to vendor (they use it to claim tax credit). Keep 1 copy. Must include: payer/payee tax ID, amount, rate, payment date, running sequence number.

5. Accumulate All WHT for the Month

Track all PND 3 items (individuals) separately from PND 53 items (companies). Separate by payment type category.

6. File & Pay by 15th of Next Month

e-Filing mandatory from Jan 2025 — Submit PND 3 and/or PND 53 via efiling.rd.go.th. Pay via internet banking / PromptPay.

🟢 KEY: From Jan 2025, ALL withholding tax returns MUST be e-Filed

DG Notification No. 451 mandates that PND 1, PND 3, PND 53, and PND 54 are all filed electronically via efiling.rd.go.th, e-Withholding Tax system, or SVS. Paper filing now requires a written explanation of why you couldn't file digitally. The +8 day e-Filing extension applies through Jan 2027.

VAT — Output, Input & Filing
Standard rate 7% (reduced from statutory 10%, extended to at least Sep 2026). Registration mandatory when annual revenue exceeds THB 1.8M.
🔄
VAT Calculation Logic
What you charge, what you reclaim, what you owe

Output VAT (you charge customers)

7% on your sales of goods/services. Included in the price or added on top — either way, you collect it and owe it to RD. Issue tax invoices to customers.

Input VAT (you pay to Thai vendors)

7% VAT on purchases from VAT-registered Thai suppliers. Keep their tax invoices as evidence. This offsets your Output VAT.

Net VAT = Output VAT − Input VAT

If positive → pay the difference to RD. If negative → carry forward as credit OR request refund (within 3 years).

File PP 30 by 23rd (e-File) or 15th (paper)

e-Filing at efiling.rd.go.th — Must file EVERY month, even nil returns.

🌐
VAT on Foreign Services (Reverse Charge)
PP 36 — self-assessed VAT when buying from overseas

Purchase Service from Foreign Provider

E.g., SaaS subscription, cloud hosting, overseas consulting, design work from an offshore freelancer.

Self-Assess 7% VAT on Amount Paid

You calculate the VAT yourself. There is no input tax invoice from a foreign provider.

File PP 36 by 15th of Next Month

e-Filing available — Note: the reverse-charge VAT you pay via PP 36 generally cannot be claimed as input VAT credit (unlike PP 30 input VAT). This is a pure cost.

⚠️ PP 36 Gotcha

The 7% reverse-charge VAT on imported services is typically NOT reclaimable as input VAT for the payer. It's an additional cost on top of the foreign invoice. Budget for this.

📋 Required Records for VAT
  • Sales tax invoices — issued to your customers with your VAT ID, the 7% amount, and customer details
  • Purchase tax invoices — received from vendors (must be proper tax invoices, not just receipts, to claim input VAT)
  • Input VAT report (ภาษีซื้อ) — monthly ledger of all input VAT from purchase invoices
  • Output VAT report (ภาษีขาย) — monthly ledger of all output VAT from sales invoices
  • Records must be kept for 5 years from the filing date
Payroll — Salary, WHT, SSO & Employee PIT
Employer obligations for each pay period and annually.
👥
Monthly Payroll Workflow

Calculate Gross Salary + Benefits

Base salary, OT, allowances, bonuses. Produce payslip (mandatory — paper or electronic).

Withhold Employee PIT

Estimate annual tax using progressive rates (0–35%), divide by 12. Deduct from gross salary. Rates: 0% up to 150K, 5% 150–300K, 10% 300–500K, 15% 500–750K, 20% 750K–1M, 25% 1–2M, 30% 2–5M, 35% over 5M.

Deduct Employee SSO (5%)

5% of salary, capped at THB 750/month (on max base of THB 15,000). From 2026, cap rises to THB 875/month (base THB 17,500).

Pay Net Salary to Employee

Gross − PIT withholding − SSO = net pay.

File PND 1 by 15th of Next Month

e-Filing mandatory from Jan 2025 — Submit PND 1 with details of each employee's salary and tax withheld. Pay via internet banking.

Submit SSO (SPS 1-10) by 15th

SSO online portal — Employee 5% + Employer 5% = 10% of salary (capped). Pay at bank / online.

The Painful English Certificate Process
Getting physical proof of WHT for foreign vendors (Ror 20/21)

1. Make Payment, Withhold Tax

Deduct WHT at the applicable rate (15% default or DTA rate) from the foreign payment.

2. File PND 54 by 15th of Next Month

e-Filing mandatory from 2025 — Report all foreign payments and WHT amounts for the month.

3. Visit Local RD Office in Person

Physical visit required — Request English-language WHT certificate (Ror 20 or Ror 21). Bring: copy of PND 54 filing receipt, company documents, invoice from foreign entity, proof of payment.

4. Receive Physical Certificate, Send to Vendor

The RD issues a stamped English certificate. Processing time varies. Send original to foreign vendor so they can claim DTA tax credit in their home country.

🔴 This is the ONE process that still requires a physical RD visit

There is no online system for requesting English WHT certificates. You or your accountant must physically go to the RD area office, queue, submit documents, and collect the certificate. This is the biggest remaining paper-based pain point.

CIT at 20% standard (SME reduced rates available). Two filings per year: half-year estimate and annual return.

FilingFormWhenCalculation
Half-year estimate PND 51 Aug (2 months after first 6 months) Half of estimated annual profit × CIT rate. Pay the tax. Credited against annual CIT.
Annual return PND 50 May (150 days after FY end) Actual net profit × CIT rate − PND 51 prepayment − WHT credits (from 50 Tawi received) = balance due/refund.
⚠️ PND 51 Underestimate Penalty

If your PND 51 estimate is less than 75% of actual annual profit (without justification), a 20% surcharge applies on the shortfall. Safe harbor: make PND 51 tax payable ≥ 50% of actual annual CIT.

Where AI vision + API integration can eliminate the tedious parts, and where you're stuck with manual processes.

High-Impact Automation Targets
Things you can build yourself, bypassing FlowAccount/PEAK
ProcessAutomation ApproachImpact
Invoice ingestion AI vision (Claude/GPT-4o) on photos/PDFs → extract: vendor name, tax ID, line items, amounts, VAT, payment type classification for WHT rate
WHT rate assignment Rules engine: classify each line item → service (3%), rent (5%), advertising (2%), transport (1%). AI can suggest, human confirms.

🏗️ Suggested DIY Architecture
  • Layer 1 — Ingestion: Mobile camera / PDF upload → AI vision API (Claude) → structured JSON (vendor, tax ID, line items, amounts, VAT, suggested WHT categories)
  • Layer 2 — Rules Engine: WHT rate lookup by payment type, vendor type (individual/company/foreign), DTA applicability. Human confirmation for ambiguous items.
  • Layer 3 — Document Gen: Auto-generate 50 Tawi PDFs, payslips, PND summary spreadsheets, PP 30 working papers.
  • Layer 4 — Accounting Sync: Push to Xero via API (bills, payments, journal entries). Or use a simple local DB/spreadsheet as source of truth.
  • Layer 5 — Filing Prep: Generate CSV/data files formatted for RD e-Filing upload. Calendar alerts for all deadlines.
